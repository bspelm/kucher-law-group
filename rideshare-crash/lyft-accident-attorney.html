What Types of Damages Can I Recover from a Brooklyn Uber or Lyft Accident?

Following a rideshare accident, the potential compensation hinges on various factors including the severity of your injuries and the nature of the losses incurred. Potential damages encompass lost wages, property damage, medical bills, and most notably, pain and suffering. If these injuries have severe implications on your ability to work or enjoy your daily life, a claim for such non-tangible damages becomes crucial.

Typically, your primary claim will target the driver responsible for the accident. However, the unique nature of rideshare accidents means you can also seek compensation from the rideshare company. Lyft accident attorney are equipped to navigate the intricate web of liability insurances held by these companies which are specifically designed to address injuries and other related losses.

Under New York laws, both Uber and Lyft mandate their drivers and associated vehicles to maintain a minimum liability insurance worth $1 million. This coverage is applicable for any damages that transpire during active duty, encompassing the journey to fetch or drop passengers. If you're a passenger and find yourself injured due to a mishap involving a Lyft driver, their personal injury protection (PIP) policy can be your immediate recourse, covering urgent medical expenses and a limited daily allowance for income loss.

How Can You Know Who Is Responsible for Your Injuries?

Even when it’s clear that a driver is at fault for your injuries, it can be complicated to determine which party or parties should pay compensation. This is because a driver who’s using the app to find passengers and accept ride requests will generally have their own personal insurance policy. On the other hand, a driver who isn’t logged into their app, or who has already accepted a ride request and is waiting to pick up a passenger, will likely be covered under the Lyft insurance policy.

Moreover, Lyft offers additional company policies that can be used to provide compensation in certain situations. These policies are often referred to as “excess” or “umbrella” insurance, and they are intended to fill gaps in liability coverage. While it’s difficult to predict who will bear responsibility for your injuries, our lawyers can investigate thoroughly and determine which parties have the necessary insurance coverage to cover your costs.
